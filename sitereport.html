My Web Development Experience

It was challenging at times but definitely a great experience learning about the basics in web development. Honestly, I scored low in HTML and CSS when the term began, but over the term, I have come to realize the various processes involved in constructing a functional and stylistically appealing website.

Site Development

For the whole term, I centered my creativity on developing a themed website that is environment-friendly. For color selection, I wanted to have green colors to represent nature and the company’s commitment to environmental conservation. The site comprises five pages: Index, Projects, Contacts, Video Demo, and Site Report.

Design Part

Color Scheme: On the issue of color selection, I chose different hues of green to give the impression of being environmentally conscious. Green connotes new growth and harmonization which are in tune with what my website is all about.

Fonts: For fonts, I decided to opt for ‘Segoe UI’ which is simple and easy to read, while for headings, I opted for ‘Georgia’ to make the design seem more classy. The home-page is designed to capture visitor attention first through a profile of myself. The use of grid layout is evident on the project page to provide a neat arrangement of the project thumbnails.

Learning: It was essential learning the structure of HTML and the styling aspect of CSS. There were several issues that I faced while designing for the web but I sorted them out using media queries.

Debugging

Bugs had been puzzling, and the process of eradicating them was always a stressful session. But tricks such as the Chrome Developer Tools proved to be extremely useful in debugging the applications.
